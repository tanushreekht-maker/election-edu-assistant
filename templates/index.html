Election Timeline

Key stages from announcement to government formation in a Lok Sabha election.

Day 0

Election Notification

The Election Commission of India issues an official notification announcing election dates. This triggers the Model Code of Conduct immediately.

Days 1–7

Nomination Filing

Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer. General candidates pay ₹25,000 deposit; SC/ST candidates pay ₹12,500. Proposers from the constituency must sign.

Day 8

Scrutiny of Nominations

The Returning Officer examines all nomination papers for validity. Documents and eligibility are verified. Defective forms are rejected at this stage.

Day 11

Withdrawal Deadline

Candidates can withdraw their candidacy by 3:00 PM on this date. After this, the final list of contesting candidates is published.

Day 12 onwards

Campaign Period

Candidates and parties campaign across constituencies. The MCC governs all conduct. Campaigning must cease 48 hours before polling (Silent Period).

Polling Day

Voting (Polling)

Voters cast ballots at designated polling booths using EVMs between 7:00 AM and 6:00 PM. VVPAT machines display a paper slip for voter verification. EVMs are sealed after polling ends.

Counting Day

Counting & Results

Votes are counted under strict ECI supervision at counting centres. Candidates, their agents, and ECI observers are present. Results are declared constituency by constituency.

Post-Result

Government Formation

The party or alliance winning 272+ seats (simple majority in 543-seat Lok Sabha) is invited by the President to form government. The leader is sworn in as Prime Minister.
